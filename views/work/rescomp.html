VISIT SITE

Working at ResComp was a very unique experience, and I learned a lot from my one year there. It was my first time working on a website that had so much content spread over so many pages. The Marketing team and I had high expectations for what we wanted to transform the ResComp brand and website into, but I knew that rolling out an entire new design would be impossible by the end of summer, when I was still getting acquainted to the code and how everything was organized.

Designing individual pages was challenging as I had to keep in mind how it would fit in with the flow of the rest of the dated website. For my summer project, I started off with a redesign of the homepage with the goal of making it more useful and approachable to viewers.

After redesigning a few more pages and rolling out a new header and footer, I began taking less caution and designing pages to be even more modern.

Takeaways

The results of my redesigns were positive and each satisfied its individual goal. However, the design of the overall site is still quite inconsistent as a result of changing the design iteratively with each page. At this point, I would create a style guide for the website as well as use a 12-column grid layout on all pages in order to start making the website more cohesive - but that is now up to the new Web Designer!
