VISIT SITE

During my summer internship at Spokeo, I got to work on a variety of different projects, including developing the UI for the Spokeo Blog, writing a script to "warm the cache" and speed up Spokeo after deployment, and a celebrity lookalike webapp.

Though I wasn't new to writing HTML, CSS, or PHP for WordPress, I learned a lot while developing the blog UI. Because the design was an extension of main Spokeo site, the majority of styles could be reused, but the SASS that I did write was expected to follow the same style guide for performance optimization and avoid any nested styles. You can read the tech post that I wrote over at Spokeo on Updating Spokeo Blog UI with WordPress.

Celebrity Lookalikes

Our facial recognition project was incredibly fun and also a great learning experience. After doing extensive research, my partner and I tested a couple of different facial recognition APIs. Our challenge was finding a service that could handle training Spokeo's huge database of celebrity photos and querying the training set in order to find the top three closest matches to an input photo.

We developed a working demo using Rails and Angular JS. The accuracy and speed of the app didn't quite meet our expectations, but I hope that Spokeo can build off the work from this project to help develop their own facial recognition feature.
